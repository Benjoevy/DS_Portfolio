About Me

I'm Benjamin Joseph Ilevbare, a Data Scientist based in Lagos, Nigeria, with a proven track record of building end-to-end machine learning pipelines — from wrangling raw, messy data to delivering clear, actionable business insights.

I've built customer segmentation models, churn-risk analyses, and predictive regression systems across retail and financial datasets. I'm a strong communicator who translates complex findings into plain-language recommendations for non-technical stakeholders.

Project — 01 · Collaborative · 8,500+ records

Credit Card Customer Segmentation & Churn Risk Analysis

Financial institutions lose revenue to preventable churn when they can't identify at-risk customers early. Led data cleaning and feature engineering on 8,500+ records, applied K-Means (k=5) to isolate a high-churn-risk cluster (22% of users) and a high-value segment (18%) generating disproportionate revenue — delivering retention recommendations projected to reduce churn by up to 15%.

Python Scikit-learn K-Means Pandas Matplotlib Seaborn

Project — 02 · End-to-end regression · 398 records

Auto MPG Fuel Efficiency Prediction

Complex interactions between engine specs make fuel efficiency hard to estimate accurately, limiting informed purchasing decisions. Built a Multiple Linear Regression pipeline on 398 vehicle records — with median imputation for missing values and one-hot encoding — achieving R² of 0.86 and MAE of ±2.1 MPG, supported by 12 diagnostic visualisations for a non-technical audience.

Python Scikit-learn Pandas NumPy Matplotlib Seaborn
GitHub Repo LinkedIn Post
🛍️
Project — 03 · Retail personas · ~200 records

Customer Segmentation using K-Means Clustering

Generic promotions leave high-value retail customers under-served and budgets wasted. Segmented ~200 customers into 5 distinct personas by annual income and spending score using the Elbow Method and Silhouette Analysis — identifying a cluster accounting for 40% of premium brand engagements and translating findings into 4 marketing recommendations subsequently adopted by the project stakeholder.

Python Scikit-learn K-Means MDS Matplotlib Seaborn
